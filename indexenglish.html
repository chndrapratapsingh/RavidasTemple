Guru Ravidas (Raidas) was born in Kashi on the day of Magha Purnima on Sunday in Samvat 1433. A couplet is prevalent about his birth. Fourteen to thirty three Magh Sudi Pandaras. Shri Ravidas manifested the welfare of the afflicted. His father's name was Raghu and mother's name was Ghurwiniya. The name of his wife is said to be Lona. [3] Raidas had gained enough practical knowledge from the company of sages. They used to make shoes and this was their business and did their work with full dedication and diligence and paid great attention to completing the work on time. [4] He acquired spiritual knowledge by becoming a disciple of Sant Ramanand. Sant Ravidas ji had made Swami Ramanand ji as Guru at the behest of Kabir Saheb ji, while his real spiritual guru was Kabir Saheb ji. used to live. From the very beginning Ravidas ji was very benevolent and kind and it became his nature to help others. He used to get special pleasure in helping sages and saints. They often presented him shoes without charging any price. His parents used to be unhappy with him because of his nature. After some time he banished Ravidas and his wife from their house. Ravidas used to do his business promptly by building a separate building for himself in the neighborhood and spent the rest of the time in God-bhajans and satsangs of sages.

Don't worship brahmins who are virtuous, worship charan chandal's who are virtuous.

Meaning-In this couplet, Ravidas ji says that one should not be worshiped just because he is in a revered position. If a person does not have the qualities worthy of that position, then he should not be worshipped. In its place, if there is such a person, who is not in a high position but is very virtuous, then he must be worshipped.

Worship the mind, the incense, the incense in the mind.

Meaning-In this line Ravidasji says that God resides only in a pure mind. If in your mind there is no hatred towards anyone, there is no greed or hatred, then your mind is the temple, lamp and incense of God. The Lord always resides in the mind of such pure thoughts.

Ravidas is not born because of birth, neither is it truer, nor is it because of poor Karam.

Meaning-In this couplet, Ravidas ji says that no person is low or small because of birth in any caste. The following make a person his karma. That's why we should always pay attention to our actions. Our deeds should always be high.

To the pure, everything is pure

Meaning- Through this proverb, once again Ravidas ji emphasizes on the purity of mind. Ravidas says, on the call of a person whose mind is pure, Mother Ganga also comes in a pot.

Be bound in the bondage of Karma, do not expect fruit, Karma is the religion of man, Sat Bhakhai Ravidas

Meaning-We should always be engaged in karma and never give up hope of getting the result in return of karma because doing karma is our religion, then it is our good fortune to get the result.

Ravidas is because of birth, Hot na ko neech, nar ko neech kar dar hai, Ochhe karam ki mud

Meaning-One does not become low just by taking birth, it is the actions of a person that make him low.

Meaning-Devotion to God is achieved with great luck. If you do not have the slightest pride, then surely your life is successful, just like a giant elephant cannot bin the sugar grains, but a small looking ant can easily bin the sugar grains.
